### Strongly recommended: incorporate first, file as the company

Before filing, **incorporate "DodoStays Ltd" (or chosen name) as a Mauritian domestic company** via the Corporate and Business Registration Department (CBRD) — 3-5 working days, ~MUR 3,000. The trademark is then filed by the company (not the individual), which:

1. Protects the mark even if Devarshi later transfers ownership
2. Aligns the trademark holder with the operating entity (avoids transfer paperwork later)
3. Strengthens the priority case (a registered company with a matching domain + filed trademark + Mauritian operating address is much harder to challenge)

CBRD online portal: https://onlinebrn.govmu.org

### Fees (Mauritius)

- Word-mark filing, single class: **MUR 2,500** (~AUD 90 / USD 55) per class
- Two classes: **MUR 5,000** (~AUD 175)
- Examination + publication: included in filing fee
- If opposition raised: separate fees apply
- **Total budget for both classes: MUR 5,000 (~AUD 175)**

### Timeline

- Filing date → priority date (immediate)
- Formal examination: 2-4 weeks
- Publication in Government Gazette: ~6 weeks after filing
- Opposition window: 90 days from publication
- Registration certificate: 6-9 months after filing if no opposition
- Renewal: every 10 years from registration

1. **Application form TM1** (downloadable from IPO Mauritius)
2. **Statement of mark** (the word "DODOSTAYS" in standard characters)
3. **Power of Attorney** (only if filed via local agent — not strictly required for direct filing)
4. **Filing fee receipt** (MUR 5,000 for two classes)
5. **Specimen of use** (NOT required at filing — Mauritius is a "first to file" jurisdiction; use comes later)

## Filing 3 (optional, defer 6-12 months) — Madrid Protocol international expansion

Once the AU registration is complete, file an international application via WIPO's Madrid System. One filing covers up to 130+ countries. **Most useful for the source-market jurisdictions** for our tourist base:

- **France** (largest tourist source — Class 43 critical)
- **United Kingdom** (post-Brexit own filing required)
- **Réunion (France territory)** — covered under FR
- **South Africa**
- **Germany**
- **India**

Madrid filing fee structure:
- Base fee: ~CHF 653 (~AUD 1,100) for 1 class word mark
- Per-country designation fee: ~CHF 100-450 each, varies by country
- **Budget for filing in 5 source-market countries: ~AUD 4,000-6,000**

Defer this until either (a) the AU mark registers, or (b) we have actual paying tourists from the target country justifying the spend.

## What this protects against

- **`dodostay.com` (singular) holder filing trademarks first** — your filing dates will pre-date theirs
- **Squatters registering `dodostays.mu`, social handles, lookalike domains**
- **Cease-and-desist threats** — registered marks shift the burden of proof onto the challenger
- **Investor due diligence questions** — "do you own the brand?" gets a yes
- **Brand dilution if the singular operator launches a similar product** — registered mark allows opposition / takedown

## What this does NOT protect against

- A genuinely-different business using "DodoStays" in unrelated classes (e.g. dodo-themed clothing in Class 25)
- The singular variant `DODOSTAY` operating in Mauritius and confusing customers — they could argue distinctness; you'd need to prove "likelihood of confusion" via brand familiarity
- Common-law rights in jurisdictions where you haven't filed
- Anyone outside the registered classes/countries

These risks are mitigated by **using the brand publicly + early** (every page on `dodostays.com` is dated evidence of use), which is why pushing to GitHub today and getting a soft launch out is its own form of trademark defense.
